General History Timeline

BT = before today

~10 000 BT

 The Demise Of The Vargfolk. As the last hope for survival vargfolk imprison themselves in orichalculum. The union of other remaining ancient races puts guardian spirits in the valley where vargfolk dwelled so that not a piece of orichalculum will "get out" of there. This is the valley which will be known as the "forbidden valley" - Laurelin village will be there.

~10 000 - 9800 BT

 Massive Battles Between Seers (the armies led by them) and Dragonfolk. The forefathers of the norsemen serve in the vast
armies of the seers against the dragons. Without the power of the Vargfolk to aid them the seers and the their armies are defeated.

~9800 BT

 The Great Flight. The seers and their armies are scattered all around the world, wandering without a permanent home. Seers will mingle with common folk of different races (although a lot of them mingle with norsefolk). The norsemen mostly travel to the continent closest to the island though - since there are 2 continents close to the island, then part of the probably become celts.

~6000 BT

 Norsemen Resettling. The norsemen end their nomadic lifestyle and build a permanent settlement near the coastline. Gorm the Wanderer & his clan settle on a land north where Gorm finds a "magical sword". This sword will be lost again later for future generations to find. This rocky land, growing to about the size of Norway (~300 000 square km) will later be known as the homeland of norsemen. The first settlement will later grow into the city of Gormsby. Apart from being blessed by ancestors this land provides good hiding places for the raiding norsemen. Dominant architecture at that time are long-houses. By this time the seers & vargfolk are long forgotten.

~4000 BT

 Cave-Forts. Norsemen establish about 4-5 strongholds in the cave-systems on the western shore where there are a lot of fjords. They don't modify the caves, some clans just use them as outposts for raiding parties. These cave-forts are well hidden and not easy to access - there are a lot of islands in these fjords and some of these islands are under water.

~3030 BT

 A War Or Feud Between Clan X And Clan Y. It can end with the death of the leaders or persons who started it (sometime after ~3000 BT then) or it can last for generations. It could also begin earlier than this.

~2900 - 2690 BT

Start of Expansion - Wave Zero:

  • ~2900 BT Start of Invasion to Southern Continent (in 2 places; peninsula being the prior one). The lands are richer there. The conquered nation lives in log-houses/-huts. Some norsemen build similar buildings, but long-houses still dominate as living houses.
  • ~2890 BT Inland tribes strike; as result - war/expansion inland, this may take about 200 years.
  • ~2830 BT Expansion N. The colder areas north are conquered. Some outposts established, but not too far north. Mainly norsemen go hunting seals etc there. And they trade with/demand tribute from the sparse population there.
  • ~2790-2770 BT Laurelin Founded. A fraction of norsemen (probably tired of the clan war or it might be connected to those norsemen who meet "celts" etc) venture to an island SW of their homeland (between homeland and the territories of "celts") to the "forbidden valley" and remain there.
  • ~2770 BT Allies and Influences. Norsemen meet a fierce and proud folk SW from their homeland - "celts". The home of the "celts" is a large island group. An alliance is formed between norsemen and the "celts". The latter become the greatest bloodbrothers to the norsemen, after some centuries they are practically known as the same nation. "Celtic" influences:
    • Money (metal pieces and jewels). It is the weight of the metal pieces or jewels that is important though. And bartering still remains too.
    • Horses (big, Shire-like warhorses i think)
    • "Shieldmaidens" aka extremely powerful female warriors
    • Archery
    • Compass (some kind of guidance stone)
    • Circle-shaped architecture/houses (used in that part of the lands later too)
    • Some changes in the writing probably

~2500 - 2400 BT

First Wave of Expansion:

  • Norsemen expand E and S
  • Celts expand S (SE from them) and W
  • Advantages: orichalculum (Laurelin comes out of isolation and mining starts) and rediscovering of ulfhedin.
  • ~2415 BT Sea-Battle In The Far-Eastern Waters With The Fire-Breathing Giant-Ships. 20 norseman ships sail further E for exploring. They meet 4 huge "chinese" ships there. 12 norseman ships survive the battle. One "chinese" ship follows norsemen to home waters and is defeated there, but manages to send a messanger-bird to "chinese homeland" first.

~2400 - 2395 BT

 The Invasion aka War With The Sea-Beast Lords. "Chinese fleet" comes to explore and take revenge. All norsemen clans unite to defeat this enemy - largest "ting" ever is held to decide this. War goes mainly on in the coastal waters and coastal regions. Nobody is losing or winning. "Chinese" just suddenly end the war by sailing away (possibly because they were called back and didn't find much interesting in these lands etc).

~2200 - 2100 BT

The Second Wave Of Expansion:

  • Invasion S. The natives there are many and they are warlike. So in this area there is always some fighting going on. There are many warcamps in these lands - both norsemen and enemy ones.
  • Celts expand SW

~2100 - 1600 BT

The Long War:

  • ~2100 BT Betrayal and Slaughter. Northern delegates are slain during one night in southerners camp. Although the was a temporary peace between norsemen and southernes, now a war breaks loose. This war is mainly about getting control over the pass or the neck of land which connects that part of the southern continent that is nearest to the northern continent with the rest of the continent S. So the "pass" is under the heaviest attack, but norsemen also raid the opposite coast.
  • ~1600 BT The Biggest Warcamp In South is Founded. This signals the end of the war - norsemen get the "pass" under their control and start planning further expansion. This warcamp is the first permanent stronghold so far south and ensures northern power over southern regions for a long time. Later the most important city in the south will evolve around that warcamp.

~1550 - 1250 BT

Golden Age Of The Expansion:

  • ~1500 - 1250 BT More Southern Regions Conquered by norsemen and celts.
  • ~1280 BT Silverfang Is Forged By A Hermit Mage. The mage is imprisoned by the guardian spirits, the dagger remains to the (Laurelin) valley for this time being.
  • ~1280 BT Curse Nr.1 In Laurelin Village.
  • ~1250 BT Norsemen Are Beaten By Some Far-Southern Folk. A group of norsemen is killed or taken prisoner there (prisoners killed later).This probably angers the norsemen at first, but at the same time they feel respect towards this nation. Opposition with these far-southerners (who might worship dragons or some other ancient race) is one of the reasons that puts an end to further expansion S. After some time some norsemen start serving as mercenaries/guards for the leaders of that land. Some of these mercenary-chiefs are given land. Today these "norsemen" speak a different language & have different customs -- they still feel some kinship to the real norsemen but don't hesitate to fight against them too.

~990 BT

 Cold Death. A great plague comes from south during one winter & kills many people in the lands of norsmen.

~800 BT

 The Great Flood. A region in the south (size of Germany) is being flooded. A large & important trading torn is completely destroyed. Ruins, treasure etc can still be found in that area.

~700 - 500 BT

 Land As A Property. In trading towns land was already sold & bought for some time. But now few clans start stressing their ownership of the land they are living on. Also more & more sons want their separate houses - so log-houses are being built too (separate houses for animals etc were built already before). Today long-houses are only some central buildings perhaps.

~500 - 100 BT

 The Time of Kings. Starts with Ulf the Grey.

~500 BT

 First King -- Ulf the Grey. He is probably an ulfhedin & well known and respected among all clans. He unites some smaller clans into one bigger one (~700 people).

~490 - 485 BT

 Ulf the Grey Unites All The Clans. Quite peacefully he manages to unite all norsemen under his leadership. This is a good & profitable time for all norsefolk.

~480 BT

 Ulf the Grey Builds Himself A Great House. This house is similar in style to the stabkirche/stave churches. It has a high ceiling & staves, columns inside & outside of the house, double wall (one outside -- it doesn't support any ceiling), many roofs on different levels, rich ornamentation. Many houses of similar style will be built by kings later -- some also make some parts of the house circular in shape (in western kingdoms later). Since the wood gets a special treatment these houses can remain up to 900 years.

~440 BT

 The Death Of Ulf The Grey. Legend tells that he died fighting a dragon. All norsemen mourned him greatly.

~440 BT

 Bjornolf The Wise Crowned. After Ulf's death norsemen are convinced that he was the descendant of Gorm the Wanderer and think that one of his sons should be their leader now. They choose Bjornolf the Wise.

~430 BT

 Bjornolf Murdered, Kingdom Splintered, Traditionalist/"Rebels" Born. Bjornolf is assassinated - probably by his own relatives or even brothers. Kingdom falls into chaos. About 20 nordic kingdoms are born + about 4-6 kingdoms which are ruled by the natives and are independent of norsemen. Some of those who were loyal to Bjornolf and those who just don't like the new rulers and ways go into exile or cave-forts etc - they are the first "rebels" or traditionalists.

~430 - 100 BT

 The Great War. The death of Bjonolf the Wise marks also the beginning of Great War - the civil war among norsemen.

~420 BT

 Western Kingdom Established. Hearn Hawkeye unites the western/celtic areas into one kingdom. Total amount of nordic kingdoms at that time is ~15. He has a pact with the "rebels", in some ways he was a traditionalist himself. So in some ways this kingdom is more similar to the ones of Ulf and Bjornolf than the other kingdoms of his time.

~415 BT

 Tombs Of Stone or Barrows. The trader-king of a eastern kingdom Gjuki Eiligsson starts a new burial tradition. Soon other kingdoms follow the tradition, only the "rebels" stick to the old ship-burnings.

~410 BT

 Western Queen Audhild the Red. Western king falls in the battle against some other kingdom or something like that. His only descendant, his daughter who was brought up in a "rebel" camp inherits the throne at the age of 13. Audhild the Red is also a "shieldmaiden". She won't rule until she is 18 though - until that the land is ruled by a council, the head of which is Ragnhall Morgansson. She conquers some more land during the first half of her rule - so the amount of "nordic" kingdoms at that time is about 10-12. She divides her country into smaller regions and makes it a law that each district must be ready to send ships and men to her (the ruler) when it is needed. Such a law is followed by many other kingdoms.

~408 BT

 New Writing. King of the Middle-Lands Njall the Farsighted develops a new runic alphabet. This will be used mainly for writing down laws etc. In other areas the runes develop in time -- some dissapear, some new ones are added etc.

~407 BT

 Eske The Sly Unites Eastern Lands. A king named Eske the Sly manages to conquer/unite eastern areas. There are about 6 nordic kingdoms at that time - 1 in west, 1 in the middle/north, 1 in east and the rest in south.

~402 BT

 Eastern Queen Brynja Dagsdatter. Eske the Sly dies and his young wife Brynja Dagsdatter inherits his throne. Many nobles and kings offer her a marriage. She promises herself to Asvald the Handsome, son of Njall the Farsighted. She breaks that promise however and marries the head of of a rich trader-family instead. Insulted Asvald wants to start a war but is his father prohibits him to do that.

~398 BT

 War Between Middle-Lands and Eastern Kingdom. Njall the Farsighted dies. His son Asvald the Handsome soon finds a reason to attack Eastern Kingdom. He loses the war against a big mercenary army, that Eastern Kingdom has hired.

~393 BT

Asvald the Handsome and Audhild the Red. Asvald the Handsome still hungers for eastern lands. He seeks union with Audhild the Red, the queen of Western Kingdom. They are victorious. But once Audhild realizes his husband wants Brynja, she kills them both and unites their realms to hers - this is the biggest kingdom anyone has seen since the death of Bjornolf the Wise. Together there are about 6-7 kingdoms at that time. Gormbsy becomes the capital of the "western kingdom", the kingdom of Audhild the Red.

~370 BT

 Audhild the Red Falls In Thorsby. Audhild the Red attacks the free city Thorsby (a large city on southern continent, still has the big stronghold that was established at the end of Long War; have to still ponder about the reason of this attack, it was probably something personal - an important family member killed or imprisoned perhaps) and is defeated there.

~350 BT

 A Far South-Eastern King Sacrified. A king who rules far south-eastern corner of the vast area under norsemen control, is killed by his own folk as a sacrifice during one especially dry summer. The king who rules after him is not his blood relative & will be known as Bjorn the Blessed since during his time fields flourish etc.

~300 BT

 Paved Streets. King of the "Western Kingdom" Audolf the Stonecutter introduces paved streets to his capital and later to the other important cities of his kingdom.

~289 BT

 Western Kingdom Divided. Audolf the Stonecutter divides the kingdom between his 4 heirs - a daughter & 3 sons. These 4 will rule the land quite peacefully for a while.

~284 BT

 Annot The Greeneyes Causes A Conflict In West. 2 of the sons of Audolf the Stonecutter fall in love with a western noblewoman called Annot Greeneyes. That causes a conflict between them. They have a duel & one dies. The other is devastated because killing of his brother, surrenders the throne & becomes a wandering hermit.

~282 - 275 BT

 Civil War In Western Kingdom. A coalition of some influential nobles sees this as an opportunity to seize power. This causes a civil war in the western countries. The treacherous nobles are also aided outside forces (some other kingdoms) and manage to kill or exile the representatives of old royal line. Together with western kingdoms there are about 14-18 kingdoms now.

~250 BT

 Rebel Attacks Intensify + Demanding Taxes. Due to intensifying rebel attacks, a South-Eastern king starts demanding taxes his inferiors in order to support the mercenary army. Other kingdoms soon follow his lead and thus tax-system is implemented.

~245 BT

 Compulsory Military Service. South-Eastern king and some others introduce compulsory military service. Before that folk was supposed to support some soldiers of their region but not all men were obliged to go to war. Now things are different - every man who can lift a weapon is obliged to go to war when his kings tells so.

~230 BT

 Thralls. Probably introduced by some Southern or South-Eastern king who needs more money to support the army and the needs of nobles.

~160 BT

 Sven The Bearstrangler Becomes The Ruler Of One Of The Western Kingdoms. He is probably a descendant of the old royal line. And his rise to power is not peaceful since he comes to claim back what he thinks is his by birthright.

~155 BT

 Sven The Bearstrangler Builds A Fort Of Stone. This fort is exceptional because it wasn't built like the cave-forts before - stone was brought to the building site etc.

~150 BT

 One Big Kingdom. Sven the Bearstrangler aims for supreme kingship & makes an alliance with those live by the old ways, the "rebels". After some years most of the lands are under his control, even some of his allies (the rebels). He also owns Laurelin now & tries to find ways how to get the "damn metal" out of the valley.

~100 BT

 The End Of The Time of Kings. Assassination Of Sven the Bearstrangler. When united kingdom falls apart, the "rebels" strike hard & soon all the kingdoms fall. The clans rule the lands again.
